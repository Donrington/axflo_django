Who We Are

Axflo Oil & Gas Limited is a wholly Nigerian-owned environmental consultancy and services company, incorporated in November 2009. We stand as Nigeria's premier provider of integrated spill-response, remediation, and environmental engineering services.

With over 48 years of combined industry experience, our team delivers proven solutions for oil spill response and remediation across all environments - offshore, nearshore, shoreline, swamp, and land areas.

Advanced Capabilities

Cutting-edge technology solutions for comprehensive environmental protection and energy infrastructure resilience.

Emergency Response Systems

AI-powered 24/7 Emergency Response Centre synchronizes satellite imagery, UAV reconnaissance, and real-time E&P data feeds to predict spill trajectories and optimize deployment logistics with zero-delay mobilization protocols.

Hydraulic Power Engineering

Proprietary 250-bar rated hydraulic power packs with load-bank testing, thermodynamic profiling, and digital twin modeling for offshore cranes, boom reels, and subsea tooling applications.

Advanced Containment Technology

Komara-20 brush skimmers achieving >95% recovery efficiency, coupled with Ro-Fence permanent booms delivering Category-5 storm resilience and IoT-enabled predictive maintenance.

Ecosystem Restoration

Geo-referenced mangrove restoration with rhizophora propagation and bioremediation protocols achieving >95% flora survival rates in previously hydrocarbon-impacted estuaries.

Terminal Protection Networks

End-to-end containment strategy for NAOC Brass & Bonny terminals featuring predictive risk matrices, pre-booming systems, and IMIST-certified response crews.

Multi-Agency Command

Tactical command centers with aerial dispersion modeling and optimized skimmer operations achieving ≥92% TPH recovery within the critical first 72 hours of incident response.

Technology Leadership

Partnering with global innovators including DESMI, Darcy Spillcare, and IPPG to deliver Tier-1 and Tier-2 readiness across all operational theatres.
